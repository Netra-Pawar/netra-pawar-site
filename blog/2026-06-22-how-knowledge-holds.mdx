This site is where I am beginning to collect articles, experiments, and research notes on documentation engineering.

<!-- truncate -->

The question I keep returning to is simple:

> How does complex knowledge become clear, usable, and trustworthy after it leaves the people who created it?

That question shows up in many places: product documentation, API quickstarts, support workflows, search systems, onboarding journeys, and increasingly, AI assistants that depend on source content.

I am interested in the repeatable parts that can be improved with systems: detecting drift, finding gaps, checking examples, measuring quality, and surfacing useful signals.

But I am equally interested in the parts that still need people: judgment, context, accuracy, user understanding, and accountability.

This site will grow slowly. For now, it is a place to explore the work of making knowledge useful at scale.
